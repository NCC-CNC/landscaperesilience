Landscape Resilience Score is the result of combining ecological variables that address themes of biodiversity, connectivity, climate, habitat, and threats at the landscape scale. Variables are min-max normalized between 0-1 and added.

Areas with high Landscape Resilience implies biodiversity, connectivity, climate and habitat are well met with minimized threats.

Landscape Resilience Score =
Critical Habitat + Species at Risk Richness + Endemic Species Richness + Common Species Richness + Species at Risk Goal + Endemic Species Goal + Common Species Goal + Connectivity + Climate Centrality + Climate Refugia + Forest Landcover + Grassland + Wetland + River + Shoreline - Climate Extremes - Human Footprint

The Contribution to Resilience captures the impact variables have to the Landscape Resilience Score within a project boundary.

The Adjusted Landscape Resilience Score divides Landscape Resilience by project area.
